\begin{abstract}
This document describes the current Lean 4 formalization in the
\texttt{BesovSpacesGoodGrid} repository.  The project develops a reusable
weak-grid framework for atomic Besov-ish spaces, proves scale inclusions,
representation-limit and compactness theorems, proves completeness for the
coefficient-cost norm, and formalizes a substantial weak-grid transmutation
pipeline.  It also contains a good-grid specialization based on Souza atoms.
The current good-grid layer also relates the standard atomic representation
norm, the \(L^2\)-normalized Haar representation norm, and the mean-oscillation
norm by finite-constant comparison theorems.
The principal public results are summarized in
Section~\ref{sec:main-theorems}.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

\subsection{Purpose of the Project}

The project formalizes part of the atomic decomposition theory from
\begin{center}
Daniel Smania, \emph{Besov-ish spaces through atomic decomposition},
Analysis \& PDE 15 (2022), no. 1, 123--174.
\end{center}
The emphasis is on a Lean library that can be reused in later developments:
the basic theory is first stated for weak grids and abstract atom families,
and good-grid/Souza spaces are then obtained as a specialization.

\subsection{Current Pipeline}

The aggregate Lean file \verb|BesovSpacesGoodGrid.lean| is intended to import
the following pipeline:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item define quantitative good grids and general weak grids;
  \item package weak-grid cells and local Banach spaces;
  \item define atom families with support, convexity, phase invariance, and
    atom-size estimates;
  \item build levelwise atomic blocks and infinite \(\Lp\) representations;
  \item define the Besov-ish space and the coefficient-cost gauge
    \verb|Norm_Costpq|;
  \item prove scale constructions and a smoothness-scale inclusion;
  \item prove representation-limit theorems, compactness statements, and
    completeness of the Besov-ish space for the cost norm;
  \item prove transmutation claims moving atomic representations from one weak
    grid to another;
  \item specialize the transmutation machinery to compare Souza atoms, Besov
    atoms, and atom families sandwiched between them on good grids;
  \item introduce Haar and standard representation gauges on good grids and
    compare them with the mean-oscillation norm.
\end{enumerate}

The repository also contains good-grid Souza-space results and auxiliary
block-sum notation.  At the date of this document the newest comparison modules
check individually, while the aggregate root module still has an import/name
organization issue involving \verb|GoodGridSpace.GoodGridCell.toLevelCell|.

\section{Grid Structures}

\subsection{Good Grids}

\begin{definition}[Good grid]
The Lean structure
\begin{center}
\verb|GoodGridSpace.GoodGrid|
\end{center}
extends the grid structure from the \verb|UnbalancedHaarWavelet| dependency.
It adds constants \(\lambda_1,\lambda_2 : \Real\) with
\[
  0 < \lambda_1 \le \lambda_2 < 1
\]
and lower and upper measure-ratio estimates for a child cell \(s\) inside a
parent cell \(t\):
\[
  \lambda_1\,\mu(t) \le \mu(s),
  \qquad
  \mu(s) \le \lambda_2\,\mu(t).
\]
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Good-grid space]
The structure
\begin{center}
\verb|GoodGridSpace.GoodGridSpace|
\end{center}
is the ambient object used by good-grid files.  It consists of a measurable
space with a fixed good grid.  The associated measure is exposed as
\verb|GoodGridSpace.measure|.
\end{definition}

\subsection{Weak Grids}

\begin{definition}[Weak grid]
The Lean structure
\begin{center}
\verb|WeakGridSpace.WeakGrid|
\end{center}
is a measure together with a sequence of finite cell families
\(\mathcal{P}^k\).  Each cell is measurable and has positive measure; the
whole measure is finite; at least one level is nonempty; and there is a
uniform same-level overlap bound \verb|Cmult1|.
\end{definition}

The term ``partition'' appears in field names because of the mathematical
source, but a weak grid does not require disjointness or covering.  The
important primitive is the overlap neighborhood
\[
  \Omega_Q^k = \{P \in \mathcal{P}^k : P \cap Q \ne \varnothing\},
\]
implemented as \verb|overlapFinset|.

\begin{lemma}[Overlap double counting]
The theorem
\begin{center}
\verb|WeakGridSpace.WeakGrid.overlap_double_sum_le|
\end{center}
states that if \(f\) is nonnegative on the level-\(k\) cells, then summing
\(f\) over all overlap neighborhoods costs at most the multiplicity constant:
\[
  \sum_{Q\in\mathcal{P}^k}\sum_{P\in\Omega_Q^k} f(P)
  \le
  C_{\mathrm{mult1}}\sum_{P\in\mathcal{P}^k} f(P).
\]
\end{lemma}

\section{Atoms}

\subsection{Cells and Local Spaces}

\begin{definition}[Weak-grid cell]
The structure
\begin{center}
\verb|WeakGridSpace.WeakGridCell|
\end{center}
bundles a level \(k\), a set \(Q\), and the proof that
\(Q\in\mathcal{P}^k\).  The theorem \verb|measurable| records that every such
cell is measurable.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Local Banach space]
The structure
\begin{center}
\verb|WeakGridSpace.LocalBanachSpace|
\end{center}
packages a complex normed complete vector space together with an injective
linear realization map into functions \(\alpha \to \Complex\).  This lets
different cells carry different local model spaces.
\end{definition}

\subsection{Atom Families}

\begin{definition}[Atom family]
The Lean structure
\begin{center}
\verb|WeakGridSpace.AtomFamily|
\end{center}
is the central abstraction for atomic decompositions.  For parameters
\(s,p,u\), it records:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*)]
  \item the Holder conjugate \(u'\);
  \item assumptions \(s>0\), \(1\le p<\infty\), and \(1\le u\le\infty\);
  \item a local Banach space \(B(Q)\) for every weak-grid cell \(Q\);
  \item a nonempty atom set \(A(Q)\subset B(Q)\);
  \item membership of local functions in \(L^{pu}\);
  \item support inside \(Q\);
  \item convexity and phase invariance of the atom set;
  \item the atom-size estimate
    \[
      \|a\|_{L^{pu}}
      \le
      \mu(Q)^{s - 1/(u'p)}.
    \]
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

The namespace \verb|AtomFamily| exposes convenience definitions and lemmas:
\verb|toFunction|, \verb|IsAtom|, \verb|AtomsOn|, \verb|allAtoms|,
\verb|atom_memLp|, \verb|local_memLp_p|, \verb|atom_support|, and
\verb|atom_norm_bound|.  It also records optional compactness and
finite-dimensional hypotheses:
\verb|StronglyCompactAtoms|, \verb|WeaklySequentiallyCompactAtoms|, and
\verb|FiniteDimensionalAtoms|.

\section{Besov-ish Spaces}

\subsection{Level Blocks}

\begin{definition}[Level block]
For an atom family \(A\), the structure
\begin{center}
\verb|WeakGridSpace.LevelBlock A k|
\end{center}
chooses one coefficient and one atom for each cell of the level \(k\).  Its
value in \(L^p\) is the finite sum over that level:
\[
  \sum_{Q\in\mathcal{P}^k} c_Q a_Q.
\]
Lean names this value \verb|LevelBlock.toLp|.
\end{definition}

The file proves that zero and scalar multiples of level blocks behave as
expected.  It also proves basic closure properties of the set
\verb|LevelBlockSet A k|.

\subsection{Representations and Costs}

\begin{definition}[Atomic representation]
The structure
\begin{center}
\verb|WeakGridSpace.LpGridRepresentation A g|
\end{center}
is a sequence of level blocks whose infinite sum is \(g\) in \(L^p\):
\[
  \HasSum\left(k\mapsto \operatorname{toLp}(B_k)\right)(g).
\]
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Coefficient cost]
For a representation \(R\), Lean defines \verb|levelCoeffPower|,
\verb|lpCost|, \verb|pqCost|, and \verb|pqCostENNReal|.  The finite-cost
predicate is
\begin{center}
\verb|LpGridRepresentation.FinitePQCost|.
\end{center}
It is the formal version of the usual \((p,q)\)-coefficient summability
condition, with the endpoint \(q=\infty\) represented by a supremum bound.
\end{definition}

\subsection{Besov-ish Space and Cost Gauge}

\begin{definition}[Besov-ish membership]
The predicates
\begin{center}
\verb|MemBesovish|,\qquad \verb|MemBesovishCoeffCost|
\end{center}
say that an \(L^p\) function admits an atomic representation, respectively an
atomic representation with finite \((p,q)\)-coefficient cost.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Besov-ish space]
The Lean definition
\begin{center}
\verb|WeakGridSpace.BesovishSpace A q|
\end{center}
is the complex submodule of \(L^p\) consisting of elements satisfying
\verb|MemBesovishCoeffCost A q|.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Coefficient-cost gauge]
For \(g\in \verb|BesovishSpace A q|\), the gauge
\begin{center}
\verb|BesovishSpace.Norm_Costpq A q g|
\end{center}
is the infimum of the \(\operatorname{pqCost}\) over all finite-cost
representations of \(g\).
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Normed-space summary]
The theorem
\begin{center}
\verb|BesovishSpace.normedSpace_and_lp_embedding|
\end{center}
packages the main structural facts about \verb|Norm_Costpq|: nonnegativity,
triangle inequality, homogeneity, a named cost-normed group/space structure,
and an explicit \(L^t\) embedding estimate for exponents
\[
  p \le t \le p u.
\]
\end{theorem}

\section{Representation Limits and Completeness}

\subsection{Analytic Grid Hypothesis}

\begin{definition}[Assumption G2]
The predicate
\begin{center}
\verb|WeakGridSpace.AssumptionG2 G s p u q|
\end{center}
combines two requirements:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*)]
  \item the coefficient-weight series needed for the \(t=p\) embedding is
    finite;
  \item the maximal measure of cells at level \(k\) tends to zero as
    \(k\to\infty\).
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

This hypothesis is used to control tails of atomic representations uniformly.

\subsection{Limit Theorems}

\begin{theorem}[Representation limit]
The theorem
\begin{center}
\verb|representation_limit|
\end{center}
formalizes the weak \(L^p\) version of Proposition \verb|compa2| from the
development.  If representations have uniformly bounded coefficient cost,
their coefficients converge cellwise, and their atoms converge weakly, then
the limiting blocks define a Besov-ish function with the same cost bound and
the represented functions converge weakly.
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Strong representation limit]
The theorem
\begin{center}
\verb|representation_limit_strong|
\end{center}
is the strong \(L^p\) version: strong convergence of the atoms gives strong
convergence of the represented functions.
\end{theorem}

The existence theorems
\verb|representation_limit_strong_existence| and
\verb|representation_limit_weak_existence| start from a bare limit block
sequence, prove it is summable, and package the resulting limit as a genuine
representation.

\subsection{Compactness and Completeness}

\begin{theorem}[Formal block summability]
The theorem
\begin{center}
\verb|formalBlockSeq_summable|
\end{center}
says that under \verb|AssumptionG2|, any formal block sequence with finite
abstract \((p,q)\)-cost is summable in \(L^p\).
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Closed cost balls]
The theorem
\begin{center}
\verb|closed_Norm_Costpq_ball_strongly_seqCompact|
\end{center}
proves sequential compactness of closed coefficient-cost balls in the ambient
strong \(L^p\) topology, assuming the compactness hypothesis
\verb|AssumptionA5|.
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Completeness]
The theorem
\begin{center}
\verb|besovishSpace_costNorm_completeSpace|
\end{center}
states that \verb|BesovishSpace A q|, endowed with the local
\verb|costNormedAddCommGroup| structure associated to \verb|Norm_Costpq|, is
complete.
\end{theorem}

\section{Weak-Grid Transmutation}

\subsection{Setup}

The file \verb|WeakGrid/Transmutation.lean| formalizes the transfer of an
atomic representation from a source weak grid \(G\) to a target weak grid
\(W\).  The source coefficients are measured by
\verb|CoeffPLevel|, \verb|CoeffPQCost|, and \verb|CoeffFinitePQCost|.

\begin{definition}[Almost linear level selector]
The predicate
\begin{center}
\verb|AlmostLinearSequence k|
\end{center}
says that the target level \(k(i)\) attached to a source level \(i\) stays
between two affine functions with the same positive slope:
\[
  r i + A \le k(i) \le r i + B.
\]
It is used to prove finiteness of certain level interactions and to reduce
coefficient estimates to convolution bounds.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Representation hypothesis]
The predicate
\begin{center}
\verb|RepresentationWsubGandALS|
\end{center}
records the structural hypothesis that every source atom has a target-grid
atomic representation with localization, decay, and almost-linear level
control.
\end{definition}

\subsection{Transmutation Objects}

For each truncation level \(N\), the file defines:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \verb|PartialSumLevels|: the source partial expansion;
  \item \verb|TransmutationCoeff|: the target coefficient \(m_{P,N}\);
  \item \verb|TransmutationAtom| and \verb|TransmutationAtomLocal|: the
    normalized target atoms \(d_{P,N}\);
  \item \verb|TransmutationBlock|: the target level block at level \(j\).
\end{itemize}

It also defines stable limiting objects:
\verb|TransmutationCoeffLimit|, \verb|TransmutationAtomLimit|, and
\verb|TransmutationBlockLimit|.

\subsection{Claims}

\begin{theorem}[Claim I]
The theorem
\begin{center}
\verb|ClaimI|
\end{center}
is the exact bookkeeping identity.  The transmuted target representation at
truncation \(N\) sums to the source partial expansion
\verb|PartialSumLevels ... N|.  The theorem \verb|ClaimI_top| is the endpoint
\(q=\infty\) version.
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Claim II]
The theorem
\begin{center}
\verb|ClaimII|
\end{center}
states that each finite transmutation block sequence is a genuine Besov-ish
representation of the source partial expansion and that its \((p,q)\)-cost is
bounded by the source coefficient cost times explicit geometric, decay, and
convolution constants.  The theorem \verb|ClaimII_top| treats the endpoint
\(q=\infty\).
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Claim III]
The theorem
\begin{center}
\verb|ClaimIII|
\end{center}
passes to the stable limiting transmutation data.  It produces an
\(L^p\)-limit \(g_{\infty}\), proves that the stable blocks form a Besov-ish
representation of \(g_{\infty}\), and proves strong convergence of the
truncated source sums to that limit.  The theorem \verb|ClaimIII_top| is the
endpoint version.
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Identity-level Claim C constant]
For the identity level selector used in the good-grid Besov-atom comparison,
the theorem
\begin{center}
\verb|LpGridRepresentation.cCoefficientInt_transmutationKernelZ_zero_one|
\end{center}
computes the integer transmutation coefficient of the Claim C kernel as the
ordinary geometric sum:
\[
  C_{\mathrm{co},\mathbb{Z}}
    \bigl(p,\infty,n\mapsto \lambda^{n}\mathbf{1}_{n\ge 0}\bigr)
  =
  (1-\rho)^{-1},
  \qquad \lambda=\rho^{p}.
\]
This is the constant simplification used in the final Souza/Besov atom
comparison.
\end{theorem}

\section{Good-Grid Souza Spaces}

The file \verb|GoodGrid/BesovSpace.lean| specializes the weak-grid theory to
good grids and Souza atoms.

\begin{definition}[Induced weak grid]
The definition
\begin{center}
\verb|GoodGridSpace.toWeakGrid|
\end{center}
turns a good grid into a weak grid.  Since good-grid partitions are disjoint,
the same-level overlap multiplicity is \(1\).  The induced weak-grid space is
\verb|GoodGridSpace.toWeakGridSpace|.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Souza atoms]
The predicate
\begin{center}
\verb|GoodGridSpace.IsSouzaAtom|
\end{center}
says that an atom is supported on a good-grid cell, is constant on that cell,
and has size at most
\[
  \mu(Q)^{s - 1/p}.
\]
The definitions \verb|canonicalSouzaAtom|, \verb|souzaLocalBanachSpace|,
\verb|souzaAtomsSet|, and \verb|souzaAtomFamily| package these atoms as an
\verb|AtomFamily| with \(u=\infty\).
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Souza Besov space]
The definition
\begin{center}
\verb|GoodGridSpace.SouzaBesovSpace|
\end{center}
is the Besov-ish space associated to the Souza atom family on the weak grid
induced by the good grid.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Good-grid completeness]
The theorem
\begin{center}
\verb|souzaBesovSpace_costNorm_completeSpace|
\end{center}
specializes the weak-grid completeness theorem to Souza atoms on a good grid.
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Compact balls]
Theorems
\begin{center}
\verb|souza_closedCostBallInLp_isSeqCompact|,\quad
\verb|souza_closedCostBallInLp_isCompact|,\quad
\verb|souza_closedCostBallInL1_isCompact|
\end{center}
state compactness of closed cost balls in the ambient \(L^p\) topology and,
after the finite-measure inclusion, in \(L^1\).
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Density]
Theorems
\begin{center}
\verb|souzaBesovSpace_dense|,\qquad
\verb|souzaBesovSpace_dense_inL1|
\end{center}
prove density of the Souza Besov space in the ambient \(L^p\) space and density
of its image in \(L^1\).
\end{theorem}

\subsection{Besov Atoms and the Souza/Besov Comparison}

The file \verb|GoodGrid/BesovAtoms.lean| introduces Besov atoms on a good grid.
A Besov atom on a cell \(Q\) is a function supported on \(Q\) whose induced
Besov-ish norm of order \(\beta\) is controlled by the scale
\(\mu(Q)^{s-\beta}\).  The construction is phrased using induced weak grids and
the transmutation API.

\begin{definition}[Besov-atom normalizing constant]
The definition
\begin{center}
\verb|besovAtomConstant|
\end{center}
is the geometric normalizing constant for Besov atoms.  The theorem
\verb|besovAtomConstant_nonneg| proves nonnegativity, and
\verb|one_le_besovAtomConstant| proves the lower bound \(1\le
\verb|besovAtomConstant|\), which is used to simplify the final Claim C
constant.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Besov atom to Souza representation]
The theorem
\begin{center}
\verb|besovAtom_to_induced_souzaS_representation_decay_claimC|
\end{center}
packages the paper's transmutation claim for a single Besov atom: after passing
to the weak grid induced on its supporting cell, the atom admits a Souza-atom
representation with geometric coefficient decay
\[
  \bigl(\lambda_2^{\beta-s}\bigr)^{p k}.
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Souza atoms and Besov atoms]
The theorem
\begin{center}
\verb|atoms_between_souza_atoms_and_besov_atoms|
\end{center}
formalizes the sandwich proposition.  If an atom family \(A\) lies between
Souza atoms and Besov atoms, then the Besov-ish spaces generated by Souza
atoms, by \(A\), and by Besov atoms are equal.  The theorem also gives the
continuous norm bounds
\[
  \|f\|_{A} \le C_1\|f\|_{\mathrm{Souza}},
  \qquad
  \|f\|_{\mathrm{Souza}}
    \le
    \frac{C_2}{1-\lambda_2^{\beta-s}}\|f\|_{A}.
\]
The second inequality is obtained by applying explicit Claim C and the
geometric-sum constant computation above.
\end{theorem}

\subsection{Haar, Standard, and Mean-Oscillation Norms}

The newer good-grid files formalize three analytic gauges associated with an
integrable function \(f\):
\begin{itemize}
  \item the standard atomic representation norm
    \verb|standardRepresentationNorm|;
  \item the \(L^2\)-normalized Haar representation norm
    \verb|haarL2RepresentationNorm|;
  \item the mean-oscillation norm \verb|meanOscillationNorm|, built from a
    global \(L^p\) term and levelwise local oscillations.
\end{itemize}

\begin{theorem}[Haar controls standard]
The theorem
\begin{center}
\verb|exists_standardRepresentationNorm_le_const_mul_haarL2RepresentationNorm|
\end{center}
states that, for fixed good-grid data and parameters, the standard atomic
representation norm is bounded by a finite constant times the Haar
representation norm.
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Finite standard norm endpoint]
The theorem
\begin{center}
\verb|finite_standardRepresentationNorm_implies_memBesov_and_standardRepresentation|
\end{center}
packages the endpoint consequence of finite standard norm.  It gives \(L^p\)
membership, the canonical standard \verb|LpGridRepresentation|, finite
\((p,q)\)-coefficient cost, and membership in the Souza Besov space, with the
expected cost bounds.
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Finite Haar norm endpoint]
The theorem
\begin{center}
\verb|finite_haarL2RepresentationNorm_implies_memLp_and_hasSum|
\end{center}
states that finite Haar representation norm implies \(f\in L^p\) and that the
normalized Haar expansion has sum \(f\) in \(L^p\).
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Standard controls mean oscillation]
The theorem
\begin{center}
\verb|exists_meanOscillationNorm_le_const_mul_standardRepresentationNorm|
\end{center}
proves the manuscript direction in which the standard representation norm
controls the mean-oscillation norm.  The proof separates the \(L^p\) term from
the oscillation seminorm.  The oscillation estimate uses the fact that
low-frequency Souza blocks are constant on finer cells, so local oscillation is
controlled by the high-frequency tail, followed by a geometric discrete
convolution estimate.
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Mean oscillation controls Haar]
The theorem
\begin{center}
\verb|exists_haarL2RepresentationNorm_le_const_mul_meanOscillationNorm|
\end{center}
proves the reverse analytic comparison from mean oscillation to Haar norm.  The
cellwise estimate is the Lean analogue of testing \(f-c_Q\) against the
zero-mean Haar wavelets over a parent cell \(Q\):
\[
  |d_S|
    \le
    C\,\operatorname{osc}_p(f,Q)\,\mu(Q)^{1/p' - 1/2}.
\]
After summing over branches and levels, this gives a finite constant \(C\) such
that
\[
  \|f\|_{\mathrm{Haar}}
    \le C\,\|f\|_{\mathrm{mean\ osc}}.
\]
\end{theorem}

\section{Conclusion}

The repository now contains a substantial weak-grid atomic-decomposition
library: it defines Besov-ish spaces, develops their coefficient-cost norm,
proves scale, compactness, completeness, and transmutation statements, and
specializes the theory to Souza atoms on good grids.  The good-grid layer now
also contains the main finite-constant comparisons among the standard atomic
norm, Haar representation norm, and mean-oscillation norm.  The most reusable
API is currently the weak-grid layer; the good-grid files show how the
abstract machinery is intended to specialize to concrete Besov spaces.
